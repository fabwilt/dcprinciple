How will artificial intelligence affect international logistics?

The emergence of artificial intelligence (AI) in many aspects of life has also transformed the field of logistics, both nationally and internationally. In recent months, AI has reached an increasingly wide audience and its impact is also spreading into the professional environment, where it could revolutionize the way logistics operations are managed.

International logistics in particular is affected by the use of artificial intelligence (AI) in both favorable and unfavorable ways. Many suggest that AI will lead to the loss of many jobs, others argue that it is a great opportunity to optimize the transport, distribution and storage processes. What can it offer to international logistics?

Process automation: AI facilitates the automation of repetitive, low- complexity tasks, such as sorting and labeling packages. This reduces human error, streamlines processes and increases productivity in international logistics.

Route optimization: AI enables advanced real-time data analysis to determine the most efficient and cost-effective routes for transporting goods internationally, whether by sea, land or air. This reduces transportation costs and delivery times, improving supply chain efficiency.
Inventory management: Thanks to AI, it is possible to make more accurate demand forecasts and automate inventory replenishment. This helps to avoid overstocking or shortages in warehouses, optimizing inventory levels and improving customer satisfaction.

Track and trace: AI enables real-time tracking of goods throughout the supply chain. With technologies such as the Internet of Things (IoT) and data analytics, accurate updates on the location and status of shipments can be obtained, improving the visibility and security of international logistics.
